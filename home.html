About Me

I'm a second-year UXID student at Drexel University. My background in fine art and coding helps me look at interfaces with an understanding of how front-end design and back-end programming work together. My goal, shaped by my past experiences with leadership and artistic direction, is to lead a team of UX designers. I want to work with a team of people who constantly learn from each other, and who aren't afraid to fail as part of the process.

I'm from Belmar, New Jersey, which is a small beach town with a population of about 5,000 residents. I attended Communications High School, a magnet school that focuses on career readiness in the communications field. I applied with hopes of becoming a graphic designer, but a mandatory beginner-level Python class changed my perspective. Initially, I dreaded it, but soon realized that I wanted to learn more about coding. That inspired me to pursue UXID, combining graphic design and web development.

Later in high school, I became the editor-in-chief of the literary magazine, National Art Honor Society president, Art Club president, and the art department leader in the drama club. These roles allowed me to experience arts-related leadership on a smaller scale. I found that my work is most fulfilling when I collaborate with equally ambitious people, and I wanted my peers to be proud of the work we did.

After transferring to Drexel from Rochester Institute of Technology, I'm glad that I made the choice to attend Drexel. I love being in Philadelphia and the proximity to other major cities in the northeast. One of the things I'm most excited about is co-op, and UXID students are able to complete three separate co-op experiences. While on co-op, I hope to learn from my colleagues and practice design in a workplace environment.
